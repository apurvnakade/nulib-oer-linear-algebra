Corollary 5.2.7. Matrix transformations.

Given any linear transformation \(T\colon \R^n\rightarrow \R^m\) there is an \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\) such that \(T=T_A\text{.}\) In fact we may take

\begin{equation*} A=\begin{bmatrix}\vert\amp \vert\amp \cdots \amp \vert \\ T(\bolde_1)\amp T(\bolde_2)\amp \cdots \amp T(\bolde_n)\\ \vert\amp \vert\amp \cdots \amp \vert \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

As a result, in the special case where the domain and codomain are both spaces of tuples, all linear transformations are matrix transformations.
