The definition of a matrix transformation below marks the first point where we take some artistic license and conflate the vector space \(\R^n\) with the vector space \(M_{n\times 1}\) of column vectors. The rationale here is partly notational in nature (\(\R^n\) is less clunky than \(M_{n\times 1}\)), and partly conceptual. We will see that the spaces \(\R^n\) come equipped with a natural geometric structure, allowing us to interpret the linear transformations among them in a more conceptual manner, especially when \(n=2, 3\text{.}\)
