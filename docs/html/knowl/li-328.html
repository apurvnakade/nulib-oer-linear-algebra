Item 4.

A polynomial on \(X\) is a function \(f\colon X\rightarrow \R\) of the form \(f(x)=\anpoly\text{,}\) where \(a_i\in \R\) and \(a_n\ne 0\text{.}\) We call \(n\) the degree of f, denoted \(\deg f=n\text{;}\) and we call \(a_n\) the leading term of \(f\).

The set of polynomials of degree at most \(n\) on \(X\) is denoted \(P_n(X)\text{;}\) the set of all polynomials on \(X\) is denoted \(P(X)\text{.}\) When \(X=\R\text{,}\) we shorten the notation to \(P_n\) and \(P\text{.}\)
