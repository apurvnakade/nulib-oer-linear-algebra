Exercise 14. Compositions of rotations and reflections.

In this exercise we will show that if we compose a rotation or reflection with another rotation or reflection, as defined in Definition 4.2.12 and Definition 4.2.16, the result is yet another rotation or reflection. For each part, express the given composition either as a rotation \(\rho_\theta\) or reflection \(r_\theta\text{,}\) where \(\theta\) is expressed in terms of \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\text{.}\)

  1. \(\displaystyle \rho_\alpha\circ\rho_\beta\)

  2. \(\displaystyle r_\alpha\circ r_\beta\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \rho_\alpha\circ r_\beta\)

  4. \(\displaystyle r_\alpha\circ \rho_\beta\)
