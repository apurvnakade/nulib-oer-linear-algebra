Theorem 6.4.3. Characteristic polynomial theorem.

Let \(A\in M_{nn}\text{,}\) and let \(p(t)=\det(tI_n-A)\) be its characteristic polynomial.

  1. \(p(t)=t^n+a_{n-1}t^{n-1}+\cdots +a_1t+a_0\text{;}\) in particular, \(p(t)\) is monic (leading coefficient is 1), of degree \(n\text{.}\)

  2. The real roots of \(p(t)\) are the (real) eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\) Since \(\deg p(t)=n\text{,}\) there are at most \(n\) distinct eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\)

  3. Let \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \dots, \lambda_n\) be the roots of \(p(t)\text{.}\) Then

    \begin{align*} \tr(A)\amp =\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_i=-a_{n-1}\\ \det(A)\amp =\prod_{i=1}^n\lambda_i=(-1)^na_0 \end{align*}
