Let \(B\) and \(B'\) be ordered bases of an \(n\)-dimensional inner product space \((V,\langle\, , \rangle)\text{.}\) Not surprisingly, if one or more of \(B\) and \(B'\) are orthornormal, our change of basis computations are simplified significantly. For example, if \(B'\) is orthonormal (or even orthogonal), the coordinate vector computations necessary to compute \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) can be done using the inner product formula of TheoremÂ 5.1.9. Furthermore, as we see below, if both \(B\) and \(B'\) are orthonormal, then the columns of \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) form an orthonormal basis of \(\R^n\text{,}\) in which case we can compute \(\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\) as

\begin{equation*} \underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}=\left(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\right)^{-1}=\left(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\right)^T\text{.} \end{equation*}

In other words, when \(B\) and \(B'\) are orthonormal bases, the change of basis matrix is what we call an orthogonal matrix.
