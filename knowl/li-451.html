Item 1.

Linearity is an easy consequence of transpose properties. For any \(A_1, A_2\in M_{nn}\) and \(c_1,c_2\in \R\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} S(c_1A_1+c_2A_2) \amp= (c_1A_1+c_2A_2)^T-(c_1A_1+c_2A_2) \\ \amp = c_1A_1^T+c_2A_2^T-c_1A_1-c_2A_2\amp (\knowl{./knowl/eg_transform_transpose.html}{\text{3.2.19}}) \\ \amp =c_1(A_1^T-A_1)+c_2(A_2^T-A_2)\\ \amp =c_1S(A_1)+c_2S(A_2)\text{.} \end{align*}
