We have

\begin{equation*} cA=(cI)A=\begin{bmatrix}c\amp 0\amp \dots\amp 0\\ 0\amp c\amp 0\amp \dots\\ \vdots \amp \\ 0\amp 0\amp \dots\amp c \end{bmatrix} A\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then

\begin{align*} \det(cA) \amp=\det((cI)A) \\ \amp =\det(cI)\, \det A\amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_det_mult.html}{\text{Theorem 3.5.25}}) \\ \amp =(\underset{n \text{times}}{\underbrace{c\cdot c\cdots c}})\,\det A \amp (\knowl{./knowl/th_det_triangular.html}{\text{Theorem 3.5.5}}) \\ \amp =c^n\det A \text{.} \end{align*}
