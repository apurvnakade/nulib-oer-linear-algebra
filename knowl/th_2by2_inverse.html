Theorem 2.3.5. Inverses of \(2\times 2\) matrices.

A matrix \(A=\abcdmatrix{a}{b}{c}{d}\) is invertible if and only if \(ad-bc\ne 0\text{.}\)

When this is the case, we have

\begin{equation*} \abcdmatrix{a}{b}{c}{d}^{-1}=\frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{amatrix}[rr]d\amp -b \\ -c\amp a \end{amatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
