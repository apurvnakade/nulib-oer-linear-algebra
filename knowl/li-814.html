Item 2.

The correspondence (5.1.1) is also useful when working in \(\R^n\) itself. Namely, there will be situations where it is convenient to represent vectors with a particular nonstandard basis \(B\text{,}\) as opposed to the standard basis \(\{\bolde_1, \bolde_2, \dots, \bolde_n\}\text{.}\) In this setting the correspondence (5.1.1) will be used as a “change of coordinates” technique.
