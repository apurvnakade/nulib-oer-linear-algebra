Theorem 3.5.16. Adjoint formula.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix. Then

\begin{equation} A\,(\adj A)=(\adj A)\, A=(\det A)I\text{.}\tag{3.5.7} \end{equation}

As a consequence, if \(\det A\ne 0\text{,}\) then \(A\) is invertible and

\begin{equation} A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det A}\, \adj A\text{.}\tag{3.5.8} \end{equation}

Proof.
