Theorem 4.5.15. Contracting and expanding to bases.

Let \(V\) be a vector space of dimension \(n\text{,}\) and let \(S\subseteq V\) be a finite subset.

  1. Contract to basis.

    If \(S\) spans \(V\text{,}\) then there is a subset of \(S\) that is a basis of \(V\text{:}\) i.e., we can contract a spanning set to a basis.

  2. Extend to basis.

    If \(S\) is linearly independent, then \(S\) is contained in a basis of \(V\text{:}\) i.e., we can extend a linearly independent set to a basis.
