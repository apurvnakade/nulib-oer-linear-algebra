Corollary 2.2.9. Failure of multiplicative cancellation.

  1. Suppose matrices \(A, B, C\) satisfy \(AB=AC\text{.}\) We cannot conclude that \(B=C\text{.}\) In logical notation:

    \begin{align*} AB=AC \amp \;\not\!\!\!\!\implies B=C \end{align*}

  2. Suppose matrices \(B, C, D\) satisfy \(BD=CD\text{.}\) We cannot conclude that \(B=C\text{.}\) In logical notation:

    \begin{align*} BD=CD \amp \;\not\!\!\!\!\implies B=C \end{align*}
