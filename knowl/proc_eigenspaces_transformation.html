Procedure 5.4.23. Computing eigenspaces of a linear transformation.

Let \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) be a linear transformation of a finite-dimensional vector space \(V\text{.}\) To compute the eigenvalues and eigenspaces of \(T\text{,}\) proceed as follows.

  1. Pick any ordered basis \(B\) of \(V\) and compute \(A=[T]_B\text{.}\)

  2. Apply Procedure 5.4.19 to \(A\) to compute bases of \(W_\lambda\) for each eigenvalue \(\lambda\) of \(A\text{.}\)

  3. For each eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{,}\) “lift” the basis of \(W_\lambda\subseteq \R^n\) back up to \(V\) using the coordinate transformation \([\phantom{\boldv}]_B\text{.}\) The result is the basis for the \(\lambda\)-eigenspace of \(T\text{.}\)
