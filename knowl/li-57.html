Item 2.1.3.b.

Now consider a more general system

\begin{equation} \begin{linsys}{2} x^2\amp +\amp y^2\amp =\amp 1\\ ax\amp +\amp by \amp =\amp c, \end{linsys}\tag{2.1.4} \end{equation}

where \(a,b,c\in\R\) are fixed constants and at least one of \(a\) or \(b\) is nonzero.

  1. Explain geometrically what a solution to the system corresponds to in terms of the graphs of its two equations.

  2. Use your geometric interpretation in (i) to argue that the system (2.1.4) has either 0, 1, or 2 solutions. Give explicit examples of such systems corresponding to each of these three cases.
