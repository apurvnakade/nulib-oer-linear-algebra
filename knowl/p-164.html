As ExampleÂ 0.2.7 illustrates, we can take a propositional function \(Q(x,y)\) in two variables and quantify one of the two variables to obtain a propositional function in one variable: e.g., \(P(x)=\exists y\, Q(x,y)\) or \(R(y)=\forall x\, Q(x,y)\text{.}\) Proceeding in this manner we engender propositions involving sequences of quantifiers. Mark well that the order of the quantifiers in such sequences is important!
