Case: \(\theta\ne \pi\).

A similar argument to the one in ExampleÂ 5.4.9 shows that \(T\) has no eigenvalues in this case. In more detail, a nonzero vector \(\boldx\) lies in the line \(\ell=\Span\{\boldx\}\text{,}\) and its rotation \(T(\boldx)\) lies in the line \(\ell'=\Span\{T(\boldx)\}\text{,}\) which is the result of rotating \(\ell\) by the angle \(\theta\text{.}\) Since \(\theta\ne \pi\text{,}\) it is clear that \(\ell\ne \ell'\text{,}\) and thus we cannot have \(T(\boldv)=\lambda\boldv\) for some \(\lambda\in \R\text{.}\)
