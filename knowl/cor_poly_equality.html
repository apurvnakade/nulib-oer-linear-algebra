Corollary 0.5.3.

Assume \(X\) is an infinite subset of \(\C\) and that \(Y=\R\) or \(Y=\C\text{.}\) If \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) is a polynomial and \(X\) is infinite, then the expression \(f(x)=\anpoly\) is unique. More precisely:

  • If \(f\) is the zero function, then \(a_i=0\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\)

  • If \(f\) is not the zero function and we have two polynomial expressions

    \begin{equation*} f(x)=\anpoly=\bmpoly\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where \(a_n, b_m\ne 0\text{,}\) then \(n=m\) and \(a_i=b_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\)
