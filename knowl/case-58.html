Induction step.

We assume that \(\det(cB)=c^n\det B\) for any \(n\times n\) matrix \(B\text{,}\) and prove \(\det(cA)=c^{n+1}\det A\) for any \((n+1)\times (n+1)\) matrix \(A\text{.}\) To this end, let \(A=[a_{ij}]_{(n+1)\times (n+1)}\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \det(cA) \amp =\det [ca_{ij}]_{(n+1)\times (n+1)} \\ \amp = \sum_{j=1}^{n+1}(-1)^{1+j}ca_{1j}\det(c\widetilde{A}_{1j})\\ \amp = \sum_{j=1}^{n+1}(-1)^{1+j}ca_{1j}(c^n \det\widetilde{A}_{1j}) \amp (\text{induction})\\ \amp = c^{n+1}\sum_{j=1}^{n+1}(-1)^{1+j}a_{1j}\det\widetilde{A}_{1j}\\ \amp =c^{n+1}\det A\text{.} \end{align*}
