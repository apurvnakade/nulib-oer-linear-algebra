Remark 4.4.14.

When using ProcedureÂ 4.4.13 to show \(S=\{f_1, f_2, \dots, f_r\}\) is linearly independent, you want to produce enough linear equations to force the unknowns \(c_i\) to all be equal to 0. Note that since you you have \(r\) unknowns, you need at at least \(r\) equations, and so must pick at least \(r\) elements \(x_i\in X\text{.}\) Do so judiciously in order to (a) force the \(c_i\) to all be equal to 0, and (b) make the necessary computations palatable.
