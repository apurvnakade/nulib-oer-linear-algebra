To compute \(A=[T]_B\) directly, we must compute \(T(\bolde_j)\) for each of the standard basis elements \(\bolde_j\text{.}\) However, for many naturally occuring transformations \(T\text{,}\) computing with respect to the standard basis is often not as convenient as computing with respect to some other basis \(B'\text{:}\) i.e., it is often easier to compute \(A'=[T]_{B'}\) for some nonstandard basis \(B'\text{.}\) When this is the case TheoremÂ 6.3.4 allows to us derive the desired matrix \(A\) from the more conveniently computed \(A'\text{:}\) namely, we have

\begin{equation*} A=P^{-1}A'P\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(P=\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.}\)
