Let \(r_0\colon \R^2\rightarrow\R^2\) be reflection across the \(x\)-axis, and let \(r_{\pi/2}\colon \R^2\rightarrow \R^2\) be reflection across the \(y\)-axis. (See Exercise 3.2.6.13.) Use an argument in the spirit of statement (i) from Remark 3.6.14 to show that

\begin{equation*} r_{\pi/2}\circ r_{0}=\rho_{\pi}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(Note: this equality can also be shown using our matrix formulas for rotations and reflections. See Exercise 3.2.6.14. )
