Exercise 7.

Consider the functions \(f(x) = \sqrt[3]{\frac{x}{7}} - 1\text{,}\) \(g(x) = -1 + 7 x^{3}\text{,}\) \(h(x) = 1 + 7 x^{3}\text{,}\) \(p(x) = \sqrt[3]{\frac{x - 1}{7}}\text{,}\) \(q(x) = \sqrt[3]{\frac{x + 1}{7}}\text{,}\) and \(r(x) = \sqrt[3]{\frac{x}{7}} + 1\text{.}\)

Which of these functions are inverses of each other?

  • \(g(x)\) and \(q(x)\)

  • \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\)

  • \(h(x)\) and \(p(x)\)

  • \(g(x)\) and \(p(x)\)

  • \(h(x)\) and \(r(x)\)

  • \(g(x)\) and \(r(x)\)

  • \(f(x)\) and \(h(x)\)

  • \(h(x)\) and \(q(x)\)

  • No pairs of these functions are inverses of each other.
