Let \(B=(\boldv_1, \boldv_2, \dots, \boldv_n)\text{.}\) By definition, the \(j\)-th column of \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\) is \([\boldv_j]_B\text{.}\) Since \(\boldv_j=0\boldv_1+0\boldv_2+\cdots +1\boldv_j+\cdots\text{,}\) we see that \([\boldv_j]_B=\bolde_j\text{,}\) and hence that \(\underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}=I\text{,}\) as claimed.
