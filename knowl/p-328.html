Assume \(X\) is an infinite subset of \(\C\) and that \(Y=\R\) or \(Y=\C\text{.}\) Let \(f\colon X\rightarrow Y\) be a nonzero polynomial function, and let \(f(x)=\anpoly\) be its unique polynomial expression with \(a_n\ne 0\text{.}\) The integer \(n\) is called the degree of \(f\text{,}\) denoted \(\deg f\text{.}\) is defined as follows:

  • if \(f\) is the zero function, then we define \(\deg f=-\infty\text{.}\)

  • otherwise, we can write \(f(x)=\anpoly\) where \(a_n\ne 0\text{,}\) and we define \(\deg f=n\text{.}\)

We call the polynomial \(f\) linear if \(\deg f=1\text{,}\) quadratic if \(\deg f=2\text{,}\) and cubic if \(\deg f=3\text{.}\)
